About Me

My Background

After leaving school I worked in a Bank, until taking time out and having children. During this time I became involved in Charity work, which I really enjoyed; but it didn’t pay the bills, so I studied and became a Social Worker. I thought this would allow me to do the work I loved while earning money. Over time I realised the system didn’t always allow me the freedom to work in a way that inspired me; so I studied and became a CBT Therapist.

I really enjoyed CBT and did it for a long time, incorporating other therapy modalities I valued, e.g. ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy) & Compassion Focused Therapy. I worked with people with an array of emotional & mental health difficulties. Then I trained as an EMDR Therapist and added that to my repertoire of interventions. 

Helping clients transform their lives by overcoming the problems that hold them back, is what truly inspires me.

I have worked in a Private London Hospital and the NHS.

People I Work With

I work with adults who are experiencing any kind of emotional upset - whether it stems from the distant past, or more recent experiences.  I have extensive experience working with trauma.  I also work with people who are low in mood, or depressed; as well as with various types of anxiety disorders, e.g. social anxiety, phobias, health anxiety, etc.  I am able to help people who feel dissatisfied with their life, and would like to change it; but either don't know how to do this, or don't really know what it is they want to change.

I am currently working towards EMDR accreditation, and I sometimes ask my clients if I may record our Sessions. This is because, in order to become accredited, I need to submit recordings of some of my sessions to my Supervisor, as evidence of the quality of my work.

Personally, if I was a client, I wouldn’t like my sessions recorded for someone else to listen to, even although I know the person listening is judging the therapist and not me. So, although I might ask if you would be willing to give me permission to record, it is perfectly fine if you decline. It won’t affect our relationship, or your therapy in any way.

Attending & Paying for Therapy:
I don’t ask clients to sign up for a particular number of sessions; and I have no contracts they need to sign; and I don’t send out Bills. All I ask is that clients pay me the agreed fee directly after each session. In cash for In-Person sessions and directly into my bank account for On-line sessions. I give receipts immediately for cash payments and once a month for On-line sessions.

I require at least 24hours notice for any cancellations, otherwise the full fee is payable.

I also require clients to attend sessions at least weekly. Less frequent attendance interferes with momentum.

As we have no therapy contract, you are free to terminate sessions at any time. I just require 24 hours notice. I would however, suggest that you give yourself at least 3-4 sessions to decide whether or not the therapy is worth the cost, because we need to get to know each other and have a good shared understanding of your difficulties before starting intervention.
