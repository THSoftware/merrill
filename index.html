From Trauma to Inspiration

Do you ever wonder how your life would be & what you would do if you were no longer confined by the difficulties you are experiencing now? Imagine being free from past painful experiences. This can definitely be a reality and I have seen it happen. It is truly inspiring to witness.

About Me

My Background

After leaving school I worked in a Bank, until taking time out and having children. During this time I got involved in Charity work, which I really enjoyed; but unfortunately it didn’t pay the bills, so I studied to become a Social Worker, which I thought would allow me to do the work I loved while earning money. Over time I realised it wasn’t allowing me to do the work that inspired me, so I studied and became a CBT therapist. I really enjoyed this work and did it for a long time. Then I trained as an EMDR Therapist; and seeing transforming changes in people’s lives, is what truly inspires me.

I have worked in The Priory Hospital and NHS. Now I have a Private Practice.

I am currently working towards EMDR accreditation, and I sometimes ask my clients if I may record our Sessions. This is because, in order to become accredited, I need to submit recordings of some of my sessions to my Supervisor, as evidence of the quality of my work.

Personally, if I was a client, I wouldn’t like my sessions recorded for someone else to listen to, even although I know the person listening is judging the therapist and not me. So, although I might ask if you would be willing to give me permission to record, it is perfectly fine if you decline. It won’t affect our relationship, or your therapy in any way.

What to do in an emergency

Please note – I am unable to provide a Crisis Service.

If you need urgent help or advice, but it is not life threatening, you can call 111. Alternatively, you can call Samaritans 24 hours a day, 7 days a week on 116 123. You can also speak to someone you trust, or contact your GP.

If you or anyone else is in immediate danger of harm – CALL 999. Or go to the A&E Department of the nearest hospital if you have time to do that.

Attending & Paying for Therapy:
I don’t ask clients to sign up for a particular number of sessions; and I have no contracts they need to sign; and I don’t send out Bills. All I ask is that clients pay me the agreed fee directly after each session. In cash for In-Person sessions and directly into my bank account for On-line sessions. I give receipts immediately for cash payments and once a month for On-line sessions.

I require at least 24hours notice for any cancellations, otherwise the full fee is payable.

I also require clients to attend sessions at least weekly, less frequent attendance interferes with momentum.

As we have no therapy contract, you are free to terminate sessions at any time. I just require 24 hours notice. I would however, suggest that you give yourself at least 3-4 sessions to decide whether or not the therapy is worth the cost, because we need to get to know each other and we need a good shared understanding of your difficulties before starting intervention.
